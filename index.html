TideWatcher Privacy Policy

Transparent privacy for coastal data and tide predictions

Last updated: July 10, 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to TideWatcher. We value your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your data when you use our app.

2. Information We Collect

Location Data

We request "When In Use" location access solely to center the map on your current position and show nearby tide stations. Location data is used in-app and is not stored on our servers.

Tide, Weather, and Station Data

We fetch station lists, tide predictions, weather, and buoy information from public sources including NOAA, NWS, and NDBC. This data may be cached temporarily within the app to improve performance.

Favorites and Notification Preferences

If you use TideWatcher without an account, your favorite station IDs and notification preferences are stored locally on your device. If you sign in, these settings are synced securely to your account in the cloud.

Account Information

If you create an account, we store your first name, last name, email address, favorites, notification preferences, and display preferences such as Weather & Moon visibility or Satellite View. Authentication and cloud storage are provided through Firebase.

App Usage Logs

If you are signed in, we record app-open activity including timestamps, date, time zone, and a running count of app launches. This data helps us understand account activity and maintain app reliability.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • Display your current location on the map.
  • Fetch and render tide predictions, charts, weather, and buoy data.
  • Save and sync your favorite stations and notification preferences.
  • Keep your display settings consistent across devices when signed in.
  • Maintain account-related usage logs.

4. Data Sharing & Security

We do not sell or rent your personal information. Firebase is used as our cloud service provider for authentication and secure data storage. Requests to external data providers such as NOAA and weather services use encrypted HTTPS connections where available.

Locally cached data remains on your device unless you choose to sign in and sync it to your account. Information may be disclosed only if required to comply with legal obligations.

5. Your Choices

  • You can revoke location access in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → TideWatcher.
  • You can manage or disable notifications in iOS Settings.
  • You may sign out anytime from the Settings screen in the app.
  • Deleting your account removes your cloud-synced profile data, favorites, preferences, and usage logs.
  • If you are not signed in, uninstalling the app removes locally stored app data.

6. Children’s Privacy

TideWatcher is not directed toward children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy occasionally. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect the most recent revision. Continued use of the app after updates indicates acceptance of the revised policy.
